# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A small private group of friends and family coordinating shared coffee orders, with Kristoffer as the primary admin. Customers visit occasionally on mobile to choose bag quantities and submit a name. The admin uses the app as an order desk: selecting coffees, opening rounds, monitoring incoming orders, closing rounds, and tracking payment and pickup.

## Product Purpose

Filtermalerlosjen exists to make private wholesale coffee ordering precise, low-friction, and trustworthy without adding account systems or unnecessary process. Success means the admin can understand the state of a round at a glance, customers can order in under a minute, and totals, pickup state, and supplier history stay clear.

## Brand Personality

Technical, calibrated, quietly expert. Filtermalerlosjen should feel like an institutional coffee ledger run by a small private guild: compact controls, useful status information, careful spacing, and a cockpit-like structure. The "losje" tone should come through as operational ritual, records, access, rounds, settlement, and protocol, never as theatrical heritage styling.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic shadcn demo styling, glossy SaaS dashboards, café lifestyle branding, oversized hero metrics, playful illustration, heavy dark-mode observability tropes, and cramped spreadsheet clones. Do not make it look like a public coffee shop website or a startup landing page.

## Design Principles

1. Treat every screen as an operational ledger: state, totals, and next actions should be immediately scannable.
2. Prefer earned density: compact buttons and rows are welcome when paired with whitespace, grouping, and hierarchy.
3. Keep components familiar: shadcn-compatible buttons, inputs, panels, and pills should do the work without invented affordances.
4. Make confidence visible: prices, quantities, supplier state, paid/collected flags, and shipping logic should feel auditable.
5. Stay small-app honest: polish the craft, but avoid brand theater beyond a concise, durable visual foundation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target practical WCAG AA behavior: strong contrast, visible focus states, readable small text, thumb-friendly tap targets on customer ordering controls, and no decorative motion. Color should reinforce state but never be the only signal.
